🧩 2×2 Twisted Corner Detector

Scan your 2×2 (Pocket Cube) with your phone camera. It works out whether a corner is twisted the wrong way — the reassembly bug that makes the cube impossible to solve — and tells you exactly which corner to twist and which way.

How the 2×2 is different

• A 2×2 has no centre pieces, so colours are learned by grouping all 24 stickers into 6 colour clusters. Your black “white” stickers just become one of the clusters.

• A 2×2 has no edges and no permutation constraint — the only thing that can make it unsolvable is a twisted corner. That’s exactly what this checks.

• It tries every possible twist and, when your cube is solved apart from the defect, highlights the exact broken corner with a coloured border and tells you which way to turn it. (Scrambled? It still gives you a foolproof way to make the cube valid again.)

• Even lighting helps a lot. After scanning, tap any square to fix a mis-read colour.
